What this webinar is about

For most people, retirement is the finish line they've been running toward for decades. But when it actually gets close, different questions start to creep in: do I really have enough to retire, and do I actually feel confident in my plan?

The truth is, saving money for retirement and knowing how to use it in retirement are two completely different skills. Taxes, Social Security timing, healthcare costs, market volatility — these are some of the decisions that can quietly cost you tens (or hundreds) of thousands of dollars if you're not prepared.

This webinar was created to help you think more deeply about your upcoming retirement and make the shift from saving to actually spending and enjoying what you've worked so hard to build.

Your Host
Kolin Hayes
Financial Planner

Hey! I'm a financial planner based in the Parkville and Platte City area, just north of Kansas City, MO. I work with clients all across the USA to help them build retirement plans that actually fit their lives.

Over the years, I started noticing a pattern. People who were 5 to 10 years away from retirement kept coming to me with the same questions and the same uncertainty. They had worked hard, saved diligently, and done everything they were supposed to do — but when retirement actually started coming into view, they weren't sure what to do next. Questions like:

  • Can I actually retire when I want?
  • How much can I safely spend every year?
  • Which accounts should I withdraw from first?
  • How much will taxes impact my retirement?
  • Should I be doing Roth conversions?
  • When should I claim Social Security?
  • Will Required Minimum Distributions become a tax problem?
  • Could Medicare premiums increase because of my income?
  • Is my investment allocation still appropriate as retirement gets closer?

I created this webinar to answer those questions. Think of it as an educational class on the things you should be thinking about, planning for, and asking your advisor before you retire. My goal is simple: to give you the knowledge and clarity you need to walk into retirement with confidence, not guesswork.
